---
id: hypotheses-are-bounded-claims
title: Frame a hypothesis as a bounded, testable claim
tags: [hypothesis, falsification, strategizer, popper]
audience: [strategizer]
---
A hypothesis is ONE falsifiable claim about the problem or system — a property
to confirm or refute — with an explicit falsification criterion, a measurable
prediction, and a prior. Frame it as a property claim, not a bet on which
method or algorithm will win.

Method-comparison hypotheses ("strategy A beats strategy B") force a
whole-campaign test to settle and resist clean falsification — they pull you
toward a single giant delegation. A property claim is testable by one bounded
experiment, and its falsification criterion maps to a single, focused
delegation flagged `is_falsification_attempt`.
